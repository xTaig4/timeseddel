# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Danish employees (funktionærer and hourly workers, HK-member types) who log their work hours at the end of a shift or workday: on the bus, in the break room, at a desk. Phone in hand, one minute of attention, often mid-context-switch. Secondary audience: the developers reviewing this repo as a portfolio piece — they judge the craft through the README screenshots before ever running it.

## Product Purpose

Track work hours, ferie/feriefridage, and a flex balance, fully offline on the user's own device. Encodes the actual Danish Holiday Act (ferieloven) accrual rules and is honest about what is law versus what depends on overenskomst. Success: logging a day takes under 15 seconds, and the balances shown are numbers the user can trust.

## Brand Personality

Rolig, præcis, til at stole på (calm, precise, trustworthy). The tone of a well-made public-sector service, not a startup. Danish-native voice throughout.

## Anti-references

- Gamified time trackers (streaks, confetti, mascots).
- SaaS dashboard clichés: gradient hero metrics, card grids, decorative charts.
- Cluttered enterprise HR suites (endless nav, dense settings trees).
- Anything that pretends legal certainty it doesn't have — no hard-coded overtime rates presented as universal.

## Design Principles

- **The numbers are the interface.** Times and balances are the content; typography and alignment do the design work (tabular numerals, right-aligned durations).
- **Log first, admire later.** The quick-log flow outranks everything else on the main screen.
- **Honest about the law.** Statutory facts (2,08 dage/md) are stated plainly; overenskomst-dependent values are labeled as configurable, never assumed.
- **Calm surfaces, one confident accent.** Restrained color strategy; the Nordic blue accent marks actions and state, never decoration.
- **Works with one thumb.** Primary actions reachable and legible on a phone held one-handed.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast in both light and dark themes.
- Touch targets ≥ 44pt for all interactive elements.
- Both color schemes fully supported (system-driven).
- Danish is the only UI language by design; date/number formats follow da-DK conventions.
